Research work

My PhD focuses on multimodal route allocation in Mobility-as-a-Service (MaaS) systems, where heterogeneous transportation services (public transport, shared mobility, personal vehicles) must be combined to generate personalized itineraries under user constraints and preferences (time, cost, emissions, comfort).

In this context, I introduced the Personalized and Resource-aware Multimodal Routing Problem (PRMRP), a formal framework integrating route composition, resource allocation, and user preferences in a unified model. The goal is to generate feasible and efficient itineraries while accounting for decentralization and privacy constraints.

My work first explored a DCOP (Distributed Constraint Optimization Problem) formulation, modeling itinerary construction as a set of coordinated local decisions. This allows decentralized reasoning while preserving the structure of the global optimization problem.

Building on this, I developed Divide-and-Coordinate approaches based on dual decomposition and Lagrangian relaxation, enabling scalable and efficient distributed optimization with limited coordination between agents.

Current and future directions include the integration of shared resources and the exploration of combinatorial auction mechanisms as an alternative decentralized coordination paradigm.
